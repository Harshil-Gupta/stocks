## Key Highlights

### India Decorative Business
- Volume Growth: 7.9%
- Value Growth: 2.8%
- Strong performance despite headwinds

### Industrial Segment
- Mid-teen growth
- Contributing to overall coatings volume growth of 8%

### Exceptional Items (Q3)
- ₹157.61 Cr total
- Labour Code implementation: ₹63.74 Cr
  - Gratuity liability: ₹52.88 Cr
  - Compensated absences: ₹10.86 Cr
- Impairment losses: ₹93.87 Cr
  - White Teak: ₹106 Cr
  - Other: ₹60.6 Cr

## Management Commentary (Amit Syngle, MD & CEO)

### On Competition
- "Market continues to witness strong competitive pressure with no signs of a pullback"
- Focusing on structural cost reduction rather than raw material deflation

### On Demand
- October saw impact from extended monsoon
- November-December showed recovery
- Q4 expected to see improvement

### On Segments
- Retail: Stable trends
- B2B: Improving
- Industrial: Strong mid-teen growth

### On Outlook
- Demand likely to remain stable in near term
- Monitoring consumption patterns in discretionary categories
- Need 1-2 quarters to assess true demand environment

## Key Concerns

1. **Profit Decline:** Despite revenue growth, PAT declined due to exceptional items
2. **Pricing Pressure:** Intense competition limiting pricing power
3. **Rural Demand:** Still recovering
4. **Competition:** From Berger Paints, Indigo Paints intensifying
